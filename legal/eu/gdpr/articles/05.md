## Overview
Article 5 establishes the fundamental principles for processing personal data under GDPR.

## Key Principles

### Paragraph 1 {#1}

Personal data shall be:

#### (a) Lawfulness, fairness and transparency {#1a}
Processed lawfully, fairly and in a transparent manner in relation to the data subject.

**Implications**:
- Must have valid legal basis (Art. 6)
- Processing must be fair to data subjects
- Clear information must be provided (Art. 12-14)

#### (b) Purpose limitation {#1b}
Collected for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes.

#### (c) Data minimization {#1c}
Adequate, relevant and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which they are processed.

#### (d) Accuracy {#1d}
Accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date.

#### (e) Storage limitation {#1e}
Kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary.

#### (f) Integrity and confidentiality {#1f}
Processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security of the personal data.

### Paragraph 2 {#2}
The controller shall be responsible for, and be able to demonstrate compliance with, paragraph 1 ('accountability').
