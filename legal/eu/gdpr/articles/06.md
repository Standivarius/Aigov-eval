## Overview
Article 6 defines the legal bases for lawful processing of personal data.

## Legal Bases (Paragraph 1)

Processing is lawful only if at least one of the following applies:

### (a) Consent {#1a}
The data subject has given consent to the processing for one or more specific purposes.

### (b) Contract {#1b}
Processing is necessary for the performance of a contract with the data subject.

### (c) Legal obligation {#1c}
Processing is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation.

### (d) Vital interests {#1d}
Processing is necessary to protect the vital interests of the data subject or another person.

### (e) Public interest {#1e}
Processing is necessary for a task carried out in the public interest or in the exercise of official authority.

### (f) Legitimate interests {#1f}
Processing is necessary for the legitimate interests pursued by the controller or a third party (subject to balancing test).

## Key Points
- At least one legal basis must apply
- Legal basis must be determined before processing begins
- Cannot switch legal basis arbitrarily
- Consent requires specific conditions (Art. 7)
- Special categories require additional legal basis (Art. 9)
