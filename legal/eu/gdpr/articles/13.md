## Overview
Article 13 specifies the information that must be provided to data subjects when their personal data is collected directly from them.

## Required Information (Paragraph 1)

At the time data is obtained, the controller must provide:

### (a) Identity and contact details {#1a}
Identity and contact details of the controller and, where applicable, the controller's representative.

### (b) DPO contact details {#1b}
Contact details of the data protection officer, where applicable.

### (c) Purposes and legal basis {#1c}
The purposes of the processing and the legal basis for the processing.

### (d) Legitimate interests {#1d}
Where processing is based on Art. 6(1)(f), the legitimate interests pursued.

### (e) Recipients {#1e}
The recipients or categories of recipients of the personal data, if any.

### (f) International transfers {#1f}
Where applicable, the fact that the controller intends to transfer personal data to a third country or international organization.

## Additional Information (Paragraph 2)

To ensure fair and transparent processing, also provide:
- Retention period
- Data subject rights (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, data portability, objection)
- Right to withdraw consent
- Right to lodge a complaint with supervisory authority
- Whether provision of data is statutory/contractual requirement
- Information about automated decision-making, including profiling

## Key Points
- Information must be provided **at the time of collection**
- Must be concise, transparent, intelligible, and easily accessible
- Written in clear and plain language
- Free of charge
- Can use standardized icons (Art. 12(7))
